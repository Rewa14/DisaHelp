Types of Disabilities

  • Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADD/ADHD)
  • A chronic condition including attention difficulty, hyperactivity, and impulsiveness
  • Agoraphobia
  • Fear of places and situations that might cause panic, helplessness, or embarrassment
  • Alcohol Addiction (alcohol dependence)
  • A chronic disease including uncontrolled drinking and preoccupation with alcohol
  • Alzheimer's Disease (senile dementia)
  • A progressive disease that destroys memory and other important mental functions
  • Anxiety Disorder
  • A serious mental illness that constant worry and fear are overwhelming, and can be crippling
  • Asperger Syndrome
  • A developmental disorder affecting ability to effectively socialize and communicate
  • Asthma (bronchial asthma)
  • A condition in which a person"s airways become inflamed, narrow and swell, and produce extra mucus
  • Autism (autism spectrum disorder)
  • A serious developmental disorder that impairs the ability to communicate and interact
  • Bipolar Disorder (manic depression)
  • A disorder associated with mood swings ranging from depressive lows to manic highs
  • Blindness
  • Complete or partial loss of vision
  • Blurred Vision
  • A lack of sharpness of vision resulting in the inability to see fine detail
  • Cancer
  • A disease in which abnormal cells divide uncontrollably and destroy body tissue
  • Cataract
  • Clouding of the normally clear lens of the eye
  • Cerebral Palsy (CP)
  • A congenital disorder of movement, muscle tone, or posture
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (CFS)
  • Extreme fatigue that can't be explained by an underlying medical condition
  • Color Blindness (color vision deficiency)
  • A reduced ability to distinguish between certain colors
  • Diabetes (diabetes mellitus)
  • A group of diseases that result in too much sugar in the blood, or high blood glucose
  • Down syndrome (trisomy 21)
  • A genetic chromosome 21 disorder causing developmental and intellectual delays
  • Drug Addiction
  • A chronic, relapsing brain disease that is characterized by compulsive drug seeking and use, despite harmful consequences
  • Dyslexia
  • A learning disorder characterized by difficulty reading
  • Hearing Loss
  • A partial or total inability to hear
  • Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS)
  • A chronic immune system disease caused by HIV (human immunodeficiency virus)
  • Hypoglycemia
  • Deficiency of glucose in the bloodstream
  • Major Depression (clinical depression)
  • A mood disorder causing a persistent feeling of sadness and loss of interest
  • Memory Loss
  • An unusual forgetfulness causing the inablility not be able to remember new events, recall one or more memories of the past, or both
  • Meniere's Disease
  • A disease of unknown cause affecting the membranous labyrinth of the ear, causing progressive deafness and attacks of tinnitus and vertigo
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
  • A disease in which the immune system eats away at the protective covering of nerves
  • Muscular Dystrophy (MD)
  • A hereditary condition marked by progressive weakening and wasting of the muscles
  • Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
  • Excessive thoughts (obsessions) that lead to repetitive behaviors (compulsions)
  • Osteoarthritis
  • The degeneration of joint cartilage and the underlying bone, causes pain and stiffness, especially in the hip, knee, and thumb joints
  • Paralysis
  • The loss of the ability to move (and sometimes to feel anything) in part or most of the body, typically as a result of illness, poison, or injury
  • Parkinson's Disease
  • A disorder of the central nervous system that affects movement, often including tremors
  • Phobia
  • Extreme or irrational fear or dislike of a specified thing or group
  • Renal Failure (Kidney failure)
  • A condition in which the kidneys lose the ability to remove waste and balance fluids
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
  • A chronic inflammatory disorder affecting small joints in the hands and feet
  • Schizophrenia
  • A brain disorder in which people interpret reality abnormally
  • Stroke (CVA)
  • Damage to the brain from interruption of its blood supply
  • Tinnitus
  • Having a ringing or buzzing in the ears.
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
  • Brain dysfunction caused by an outside force, usually a violent blow to the head
  • Tuberculosis (TB)
  • A potentially serious infectious bacterial disease that mainly affects the lungs
