Disability Support
Jimmy

What you Can do

  • Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADD/ADHD)
    • -Keep things in perspective, actions are not always intentional
    -Don't worry about the small stuff
    -Be ready to compromise
    -Stay in a positive demeanor
    -Join a support group
    -Be able to take a break and have your child be taken care of a family member
    -Have a set routine
  • Agoraphobia
    • -Write down most and least frightening scenarios
    -Write down small steps you can take
    -Write down good things that has happened to you recently and/or share them with others
    -Relax, meditate and once relaxed imagine yourself in your worst scenario
    -Watch funny videos, laughter is your best medicine
    -Learn a new skill
  • Alcohol Addiction (alcohol dependence)
    • -Counseling
    -Support group
    -Psychotherapy (Group, Individual)
    -Therapy (Behavior, Family, Aversion, Cognitive Behavioral)
  • Alzheimer's Disease (senile dementia)
    • -Don't forget to take any necessary medications
    -Stay up to date in information by signing up for newsletters
    -Advocate
    -Do physical exercise
  • Anxiety Disorder
    • -Don't forget to take any necessary medications if it helps
    -Recognize your symptoms and do counter actions
    -Relax, do yoga and meditate
    -Do something that you enjoy doing
    -Avoid alcohol and caffeine
    -Imagine things that have triggered anxiety in the past and once comfortable then to them
  • Asperger Syndrome
    • -Break complicated processes into smaller steps
    -Social skills training
    -Stay tolerant
    -Take control of your emotions by analysing changes as they happen
    -Reinforce good behavior
    -Don't forget to take any necessary medications
  • Asthma (bronchial asthma)
    • -Monitor your lung function
    -Stay away from anything that can or could kick up dust, ie vacuuming, pets, etc
    -Don't Smoke
  • Autism (autism spectrum disorder)
    • -Start any treatment once there are early signs of autism
    -Become an expert on autism, learn all unobjective information you can
    -Set routine
    -Set a safety zone where your child can be themselves, ie your house
    -Look for nonverbal cues
    -Join a support group
  • Bipolar Disorder (manic depression)
    • -Learn all you can about Bipolar Disorder
    -Ask others for help if you feel you need it
    -Follow your doctor's medical advice to the dot
    -Lean what your triggers are and create counter actions that you can do
    -Have easy access to medicine, ie in your car, in your purse etc
    -Don't listen to your negative thoughts, try to block them out
    -Avoid alcohol
  • Blindness
    • -Join a blindness service such as Guide Dogs for the Blind or somewhere to teach you skills of blindness
    -Braille is not that hard to learn and is very helpful
    -Remember that you can still do things you love just in a different way
    -Don't forget about food labels such as stickers on fruit
  • Blurred Vision
    • -Get reading glasses (prescription or just off the rack)
    -Have lasik surgery if your eye doctor recommends it
    -Take eye exams based on when your eye doctor recommends
  • Cancer
    • -Don't blame yourself
    -Learn everything you can but avoid message boards and objective information
    -Keep in touch with friends and family
    -Find a goo doctor that you can connect with
  • Cataract
    • -Constanty wear eye protection such as sunglasses
    -Wear hats that can block out the sun, ie visor, sunhat, baseball hat etc.
    -Moderate your smoking and alcohol consumption if you smoke or drink
    -Eat lots of fruits and vegatables
    -As your doctor if surgery is right for you
  • Cerebral Palsy (CP)
    • -Start any medical treatments as young as possible
    -Ask your doctor if surgery of braces would be the most helpful
    -Have a physical therapist, occupational therapist, and speech therapy therapist
  • Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (CFS)
    • -Your mind and body are equally as important
    -Keep family and friends informed on your status
    -Write down your pre and post states, such as your schedule
    -Get a therapist
    -Avoid stress, caffeine, alcohol, heat, and sugars
    -Eat small regulars meal to avoid nausea
    -Try to avoid standing still for long periods of time
  • Color Blindness (color vision deficiency)
    • -Get a diagnosis on what type you have from a doctor
    -Take a home test
    -See if colorblind glasses are right for you (a tinted type of sunglasses)
  • Diabetes (diabetes mellitus)
    • -Stick to a routine
    -Record your blood pressure during each doctors visit
    -Record your cholesterol once a year
    -Record your A1C at least twice a year
    -Make sure you wash your hands before you prick your finger to record your blood glutose levels
    -Stay or become physically active
    -You should fast for about 10 hours before you take a blood test
    -If you have problems sleeping, drink a cup of hot herbal tea half an hour before you go to bed
  • Down syndrome (trisomy 21)
    • -Get involved in a support group
    -Be consistent
    -Think big picture
    -When teaching use different approaches that works
    -Focus on body language and behavior for communication
  • Drug Addiction
    • -Visit a Narcotics Anonymous meeting
    -Call 1-800-662-HELP in the U.S.
    -Talk to Family Members, close friends, other recovering addicts, etc
    -Have conversations often
  • Dyslexia
    • -Visit a Dyslexia Action Learning Centres
    -Take a Diagnostic assessment or a screening interview to see how you are
    -Speak to your school/college SENCO or inclusion manager
    -Read about dyslexia
  • Hearing Loss
    • -Take a hearing test online
    -Talk to your family and friends about your difficulty
    -Check with your GP
    -Learn sign language or see if you can have a hearing aid
  • Acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS)
    • -Join the UN Foundation
    -Talk with others who have been diagnosed as being HIV-positive.
    -Find groups such as message boards or support groups
  • Hypoglycemia
    • -Keep a constant level of glucose in your blood
    -ELearn your symptomsat or drink about 10-20g of sugar each day
    -Learn your symptoms and keep track of them
    -Test your blood sugar levels daily
  • Major Depression (clinical depression)
    • -Don’t make any major life decision while in a depressed state
    -Talk to trusted friends and family members
    -Keep up with social activities even if you don’t feel like it
    -Keep a negative thought log
  • Memory Loss
    • -Avoid alcohol
    -Stay mentally active by doing crossword puzzles, read more, etc
    -Socialize regularly
    -Get a good nights sleep
    -Eat healthy
  • Meniere's Disease
    • -During an attack take your medication quickly and grab the nearest support and stay in a stable position
    -For tinnitus use sound therapy and anything that relaxes you
    -You can have surgery for major attacks of vertigo
    -There are also support groups you can join
  • Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
    • -Avoid heat
    -Combine mental and physical exercise
    -Keep your life simple and stay away from stress
    -Talk to your family and friends
    -Keep your life happy
  • Muscular Dystrophy (MD)
    • -Take creatine supplements
    -Surgery or a dietitian could help you swallow
    -Have your heart assessed regularly
    -Do range-of-motion exercises to loosen joints or use a brace
  • Obsessive Compulsive Disorder (OCD)
    • -Focus against the thoughts
    -Talk to a therapist
    -Educate yourself
    -Talk to friends and family
  • Osteoarthritis
    • -Eat foods with Omega 3 fats
    -Rest often but exercise lightly to keep joints moving
    -Keep heat on joints
  • Paralysis
    • -Get an orthos (a brace for specific limbs) or a wheelchair
    -Use assistive technology
    -Redesign your schedule
  • Parkinson's Disease
    • -Take medication carefully
    -Do regular exercise
    -Visit your doctor regularly
  • Phobia
    • -Think of steps you can take to face your phobia
    -Write down most and least frightening scenarios
    -Arrange fears from greatest to least
    -Relax, meditate and once relaxed imagine yourself in your worst scenario
    -Don't listen to your negative thoughts, try to block them out
    -Talk to a therapist
  • Renal Failure (Kidney failure)
    • -Keep an active diet that keep blood pressure at your doctor recommended target
    -Take steps to stop smoking
    -Stay or become physically active
    -Eat healthier
  • Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
    • -Develop healthy habits
    -Get enough sleep
    -Get assistive devices
    -Take medicine on a schedule
  • Schizophrenia
    • -Increase your intake of omega 3 and 6 fats
    -Keep a healthy diet
    -Talk with your family
  • Stroke (CVA)
    • -Change your life styles
    -Keep a low blood pressure
    -Drug treatment in the early hours after stroke may also help some people.
  • Tinnitus
    • -Wear earplugs
    -Moderate your smoking and alcohol consumption if you smoke or drink
    -Stay away from stressful events
  • Traumatic Brain Injury
    • -Get a therapist
    -If you notice slight memory loss don’t think it insignificant
    -If anything unusual changes talk to your doctor
  • Tuberculosis (TB)
    • -Take the necessary medication
    -Don’t stop medication too soon
    -Have your sputum analysed weekly
